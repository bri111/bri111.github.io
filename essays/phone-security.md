## HON 101: Research Literature Review
In the 21st century, mobile phones are miniature computers carried in a person’s pocket or purse. These phones allow us to connect to our families, friends, and the world. They link us to our banks, shopping carts, games, and documents that are stored on our phones. With these newer technologies, security and privacy issues are on the rise. It is common to have antivirus software on our laptops and desktops but not so much on our mobile phones. Smaller storage and our phone company’s protection with 2-factor authentication seem to do the trick for most mobile users. Recent studies have shown that malware and security issues occur more frequently on android phones. Addressing these problems is the first step but why is this occurring and what security measures are already in place?

The entire research paper started off with learning about antivirus software in personal computers. Reading Kevin Collier’s NBC news article about the worth of antivirus in computers got me thinking about the manufacturer’s responsibility to provide security for each and every one of their users. This eliminates competition from antivirus software companies such as McAfee, Norton, and Webroot. In “Evalulation of Security and Privacy of Smartphone Users,” compared the “two most important smart phone operating systems-Android and iOS.” An important part about this research is understanding the differences between the two operating systems and why both systems are unique. The compare and contrast in this article seemed to favor iOS devices due to the known security features that Apple has while Google’s open source android system needs to have some tweeks. 

In 2015, Khandelwal et al explored the security system vulnerabilities in android devices. The article describes the security architecture of an Android phone by diving into the privilege separation, permissions, and application code signing. These are three major components of an android security system that enables some protection.  The authors list security measures that help protect users on their mobile phones from attacks. Antimalware, firewall, intrusion systems etc. are all similar to computer security systems. Essentially this article touches briefly on security details that an android phone has which allows me to understand and manuver what is already in place and what can be added on. 

Attached to security aspect of a mobile phone, permissions and what each app does to communicate with each other are at times sophisticated and complex. Allowing apps to talk to each other such as connecting your photos and videos to a video editing app can be very beneficial but permissions freely given can lead to consequences. This article’s results proposes a comprehensive security against apps and encrypted files as well as demonstrating the lack of knowledge users possessive over their private information (Ngobeni). In another article, the researchers implemented an attack scenario by compromissing the GPS corrdinates prvicay of a user. Their prediction was that smart phones were going to outnumber the world’s total population by 2017 and that Android devices tend to be more programmer friendly rather than restrictive (Dar). 

In all of these articles, they gave me a deeper insight into what security systems were in place in smart phones, what types of malware attacks are out there, and various solutions to certain security issues. Android devices, being another and cheaper outlet to enter the world of cellphones, has it’s advantages and disadvantages. Companies and individuals should learn and educate themselves in order to prevent threats. The strenghts of these articles were shown in the extensive research done on what are android security systems implemented but to solve the universal problem of cyber threats, that is yet to be discovered. The next steps in my research is implementing various tools or ideas into creating a universal android security operating system.
