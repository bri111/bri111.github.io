<h2>January 24, 2023</h2>
<img class="ui medium left floated image" src="https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/oNLJzwArPTPtGsvPomLex7-415-80.jpg" alt="Working from Home">
The date is January 24, 2023 and I am two weeks into my sixth semster. While this log is pretty late (I had plans on writing this over winter break, but things got too busy), better late than never :). I'm not going to talk with all the flowery language here because, quite frankly, it's boring! Anyway, I have recently fell back in love with data science. Having just finished two rigorous upper-level courses focused on data analysis and data visualization, I remembered why I decided to pursue this career track. For some odd reason, it's so easy to hyperfocus on data science projects when, comparably, it's like pulling teeth trying to get myself to pursue other topics. My guess is that I enjoy data science because, while it is mainly done through coding and statistics, I can apply it to a wide variety of fields and domains. Just last semester I built a classification forest on cancer malignancy! The dataset wasn't every extensive, but it was still exciting to be actively building models and performing my own analysis. As of right now, I'm pretty stressed with the deadlines and the projects that are expected of me these upcoming semesters (hello graduation!), but my previous mentor and director always said, "If you're not at least a little stressed before every performance, you'll end up boring the crowd. Look alive people!"

<h2>May 12, 2022</h2>
<img class="ui medium right floated image" src="../images/software-engineer.jpeg" alt="Cartoon Software Engineer">
I have decided to make this a log-style post to document my current professional status and future career plans. As you can tell, the date is May 12, 2022. I have just finished with my fourth semester as a student at the Unversity of Hawaii at Manoa as an undergraduate studying computer science. Having juggled an immense amount of responsibility and pressure with keeping two part-time jobs down in addition to my main priority of schooling, I have learned (the hard way) not to stretch myself too thin. This semester was brutal, and it was almost entirely my fault in bringing too much obligations into my schedule. As a result, my grades suffered and (while grades haven't been posted at the time of this entry) I suspect that I have two B's. Although not bad, it definitely isn't my best work.

In relation to my previous goals and aspirations, I've grown to (somewhat) dislike web design. I have infinite amounts of respect for web designers who make their job look effortless because (at least for me) their job is quite hard! While being able to program stunning visuals is undoubtedly very useful, I don't experience the same passion for learning with web design as I do about other computer science topics, such as data analysis, data management, and coding lagnuages. Upon considering everything I've learned thus far, I can safely say that while my passion for more "technical" aspects of computer science has not waned, my interest in the more "creative" fields of computer science has lowered for the time being. However, after taking ICS 212 (Program Struture), I have begun to become more interested in language interoperability and code from different languages can be combined in asingle project to achieve great results.

<h2>January 20, 2022</h2>
<img class="ui medium left floated image" src="../images/technology-cartoon.jpeg" alt="Technology Symbols Cartoon">
My goal as an aspiring computer science professional is to become proficient in web design and data science. I've always been more drawn to the creative side of programming; I enjoy seeing what visuals and asthetics I can bring to life using simple text commands. A cohesive website design is always pleasing to look at, and I enjoy that feeling of satisfaction when you can see your the fruits of your labor working with no errors for anyone to see. As for data science, I don't really know how to put it into words. I just like the feeling of analyzing raw numbers and data and hopefully gaining some meaning out of it.

As part of my journey, I hope to learn a wide variety of programming languages. In doing so, I can learn which language performs best in a certain situation, and tailor which language to use in any given situation. I also want to develop tolerance to work-related stress, specifically with deadlines. Speaking from experience, a ticking clock or hard deadlines have always stressed me out a little too much, so I aim to work on that as I develop my career.

I also hope to experience designing a website from start to finish. Learning how to code a dynamic but user-friendly website sounds like a fun challenge, if I'm being honest. I also want to expand my computational skills and learn how to best analyze large datasets to provide new insight. Most of all, I want to see how techonology develops from its current state to 10, even 20 years in the future. How will things have changed? What will become the new normal? Overall, I can safely say that I am excited to learn and develop my skills as a programmer as I learn more about the field of computer science.
